2. The Hidden Circle

3. The Double-Time Trap

A population of bacteria triples every 4 hours. If there are 100 bacteria initially, which equation represents the population \(P\) after \(t\) hours?

ðŸš© The Trap

Writing \(P = 100(3)^{4t}\). Most students put the rate in the wrong place.

âœ… The Practix Shortcut

The "Growth Rule": The exponent is always (Total Time / Interval Time).

\[ P = 100(3)^{t/4} \]

If \(t = 4\), the exponent becomes 1 (tripled once). If \(t = 8\), it becomes 2 (tripled twice). Logic beats memorization.

4. The System Illusion

ðŸš© The Trap

Solving for \(x\), then solving for \(y\), then multiplying by 11. That's a 3-step process. You only have 60 seconds.

âœ… The Practix Shortcut

Add the entire system. Sometimes the test makers give you a "gift" if you look at the structure vertically.

(5x - 3y) + (6x + 3y) = 10 + 23
11x = 33

The question asked for \(11x\). You are already at the finish line. Answer: 33.

5. The Parameter Puzzle

The equation \(x^2 + kx + 9 = 0\) has exactly one real solution. If \(k > 0\), what is the value of \(k\)?

ðŸš© The Trap

Plugging in numbers for \(x\) and \(k\) and hoping to find a pattern. This is a purely conceptual question.

âœ… The Practix Shortcut

Exactly one solution means the Discriminant is ZERO (\(b^2 - 4ac = 0\)).

\[ k^2 - 4(1)(9) = 0 \] \[ k^2 = 36 \] \[ k = 6 \text{ (since } k > 0) \]

Answer: 6. Master the discriminant, and these "hard" questions become free points.
