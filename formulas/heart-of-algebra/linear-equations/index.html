Slope-Intercept Form

\[ y = mx + b \]

Where \(m\) is the slope (rate of change) and \(b\) is the y-intercept (initial value).

Example Problem:

"A plumber charges a fix fee of $50 plus $30 per hour. Which equation represents the total cost \(C\) for \(h\) hours?"

Solution: \(C = 30h + 50\)

The Constant Trick

\[ k = \frac{y}{x} \]

For direct variation/proportionality, the ratio remains constant. Use this instead of full equations.

The Practix Way:

If \(y=4\) when \(x=8\), what is \(y\) when \(x=20\)?

Ratio is \(4/8 = 0.5\). So \(y = 20 \times 0.5 = 10\). Done in 2s.
