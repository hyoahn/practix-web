The Translation Keys

English Keyword Math Symbol Role in \(y = mx + b\)
"Per", "Each", "Every" Multiplication (\(\cdot\)) The Slope (\(m\))
"Initial", "Flat fee", "Start" Addition (\(+\)) The Y-Intercept (\(b\))
"Is", "Total", "Results in" Equals (\(=\)) The Output (\(y\))

Practice: "Translate 'A data plan costs $10 per GB plus a $30 flat fee' into an equation."

Show Solution & Analysis
🚫 School Way (Rigorous)

1. Define variables:
Let \(x\) = GB used. Let \(y\) = Total cost.
2. Build Equation:
Cost = (Rate $\times$ Usage) + Fee
Result: \(y = 10x + 30\)

✅ Practix Way (Optimal)

**Step 1:** Spot the 'per'. That's the slope.
\(m = 10\).
**Step 2:** Spot the 'flat fee'. That's the y-intercept.
\(b = 30\).
**Result: \(y = 10x + 30\)**
Mapping keywords to \(m\) and \(b\) is the fastest path.

Interpreting Slope (\(m\))

\[ m = \text{Rate of Change} \]

Slope is always the amount added or subtracted for every single increase in \(x\).

Practice: "In \(C = 0.50x + 20\), where \(C\) is cost and \(x\) is minutes, what does **0.50** represent?"

Show Solution & Analysis
🚫 School Way (Rigorous)

1. Pattern Match:
Compare to \(y = mx + b\). \(m = 0.50\).
2. Define \(m\):
Slope is change in cost over change in minutes.
Result: Cost per minute

✅ Practix Way (Optimal)

**Step 1:** Is it multiplying the variable?
Yes. That's the **Rate**.
**Step 2:** Units are (Output) / (Input).
Cost / Minute.
**Result: Cost per minute**
Slope = "How much for ONE more?"

Interpreting Intercept (\(b\))

\[ b = \text{Initial Value} \]

The y-intercept is the value when the input is zero. Think "starting line".

Practice: "In \(H = 3t + 5\), where \(H\) is height and \(t\) is years, what does **5** represent?"

Show Solution & Analysis
🚫 School Way (Rigorous)

1. Find intercept:
The constant term is 5.
2. Interpret:
At \(t = 0\), \(H = 5\).
Result: Initial height

✅ Practix Way (Optimal)

**Step 1:** Constant Check.
It's the lonely number. That's the **Start**.
**Step 2:** Match units.
Height units.
**Result: Initial height**
Intercept = "How much before we start the clock?"
