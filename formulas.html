The 12 Formulas They Don't Give You on the SAT

The College Board provides a basic reference sheet (area, volume, triangles). But they leave out the shortcuts you actually need for an 800. Here's the complete list.

What IS on the Official Reference Sheet?

The Digital SAT gives you these formulas:

  • Area formulas (circle, rectangle, triangle)
  • Volume formulas (cylinder, sphere, cone, pyramid)
  • Special right triangles (30-60-90, 45-45-90)
  • Pythagorean theorem: \(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\)

That's it. No algebra. No coordinate geometry. No shortcuts.

The 12 Missing Formulas You Must Memorize

üìê Category 1: Quadratic Shortcuts (The Biggest Time Savers)

1. Vertex Formula (x-coordinate)

\[ x = -\frac{b}{2a} \]

When to use: "What is the maximum height?" or "At what value of x does the minimum occur?"

Example: For \(f(x) = 3x^2 - 18x + 7\), the vertex x-coordinate is \(-(-18)/(2¬∑3) = 3\)

2. Sum of Solutions (Roots)

\[ \text{Sum} = -\frac{b}{a} \]

When to use: "What is the sum of the solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\)?"

Example: For \(3x^2 - 18x + 7 = 0\), sum = \(-(-18)/3 = 6\)

Never use the quadratic formula for this question type. This is instant.

3. Product of Solutions

\[ \text{Product} = \frac{c}{a} \]

When to use: "What is the product of the roots?"

Example: For \(3x^2 - 18x + 7 = 0\), product = \(7/3\)

4. The Discriminant (Number of Solutions)

\[ \Delta = b^2 - 4ac \]

When to use: "How many real solutions does this quadratic have?"

  • If \(\Delta > 0\): 2 real solutions
  • If \(\Delta = 0\): 1 real solution (repeated root)
  • If \(\Delta < 0\): No real solutions

üìç Category 2: Coordinate Geometry Essentials

5. Circle Equation (Standard Form)

\[ (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2 \]

What it means: Center at \((h, k)\), radius = \(r\)

Example: \((x - 3)^2 + (y + 2)^2 = 25\) ‚Üí Center: \((3, -2)\), Radius: \(5\)

6. Distance Formula

\[ d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2} \]

When to use: "What is the distance between points A and B?"

Example: Distance between \((2, 3)\) and \((5, 7)\) = \(\sqrt{(5-2)^2 + (7-3)^2} = \sqrt{9+16} = 5\)

7. Midpoint Formula

\[ M = \left( \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2} \right) \]

When to use: "What is the midpoint of segment AB?"

Shortcut: Just average the x's, average the y's.

üìà Category 3: Growth, Decay & Percent Change

8. Exponential Growth/Decay

\[ y = a(1 \pm r)^t \]

Variables: \(a\) = initial amount, \(r\) = rate (as decimal), \(t\) = time

Example: $1000 invested at 5% annual growth for 3 years: \(1000(1.05)^3 = \$1157.63\)

9. Percent Change

\[ \text{Percent Change} = \frac{\text{New} - \text{Old}}{\text{Old}} \times 100 \]

When to use: "By what percent did the price increase?"

Example: Price went from $50 to $65: \((65-50)/50 √ó 100 = 30\%\) increase

10. Sine

\[ \sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} \]

11. Cosine

\[ \cos(\theta) = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} \]

12. Tangent

\[ \tan(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}} \]

How to Actually Memorize These

Don't just read this list once. Use these strategies:

  1. Make a physical cheat sheet: Write all 12 on one index card. Carry it everywhere for 1 week.
  2. Flashcard drill: Use Anki or Quizlet. Quiz yourself daily until you can recite them instantly.
  3. Practice problems: Don't just memorize formulas. Use them on real SAT questions so you recognize when to apply each one.
  4. Teach someone else: Explain these formulas to a friend or study group. Teaching = deep learning.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I bring my own formula sheet to the SAT?

No. You must memorize these formulas. The only reference sheet allowed is the one provided digitally on-screen during the test.

Will I actually need all 12 of these?

On any given SAT, you'll probably see 8-10 of these. But you don't know which 8-10, so memorize all 12. The quadratic shortcuts (#1-4) appear on nearly every test.

What if I forget one during the test?

Some of these can be derived. For example, the distance formula is just the Pythagorean theorem in disguise. But deriving formulas wastes time. Better to memorize them cold.
