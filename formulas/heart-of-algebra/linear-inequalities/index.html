Boss Mode

The Negative Flip

When you multiply or divide both sides of an inequality by a negative number, you MUST flip the inequality symbol.

\[ -2x < 10 \implies x> -5 \]

Practice: Solve for \(x\): \( -3x + 4 \geq 19 \)

Practix Way

Instead of dividing by a negative and risking a flip error, add the variable term to the other side to make it positive. Zero risk, same result.

Speed Hack

Shading & Boundaries

Don't pick test points. Use the visual shortcut for isolated \(y\) forms:

  • \(y > mx + b\): Shade ABOVE the line.
  • \(y < mx + b\): Shade BELOW the line.
  • \(\geq, \leq\): Use a SOLID line (boundary included).
  • \(>, <\)< /strong>: Use a DASHED line (boundary excluded).

Practice: For \( y \leq -2x + 4 \), should the shading be above or below, and is the line solid or dashed?
