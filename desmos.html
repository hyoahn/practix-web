Frequently Asked Questions

Is Desmos allowed on the SAT?

Yes! The Desmos graphing calculator is built directly into the Digital SAT. You don't need to bring your own calculator‚Äîit's provided on-screen for all students taking the digital version of the test.

What does the tilde (~) mean in Desmos?

The tilde (~) is used for regression analysis. When you type y‚ÇÅ ~ m¬∑x‚ÇÅ + b, Desmos finds the values of m and b that best fit your data points. It's different from the equals sign (=) which just plots a specific equation.

Can I use Desmos on the paper SAT?

No. Desmos is only available on the Digital SAT. If you're taking the paper version (still offered at some international locations), you'll need a traditional graphing calculator like the TI-84.

How do I type subscripts in Desmos?

Just type the number after the variable. Desmos automatically formats x1 as x‚ÇÅ and y1 as y‚ÇÅ. For the tilde, it's usually above the Tab key on most keyboards (Shift + `).
