Quadratics

1. The Vertex Shortcut

\[ x = -\frac{b}{2a} \]

On the SAT, you'll constantly see parabolas. Instead of spending 2 minutes "completing the square" to find the peak (maximum) or valley (minimum), use this ratio to find the center line instantly.

"A ball is thrown in the air. Its height h after t seconds is modeled by \(h(t) = -5t^2 + 20t + 5\). At what time does the ball reach its maximum height?"

Complete the square:
\(h(t) = -5(t^2 - 4t) + 5\)
\(h(t) = -5(t-2)^2 + 25\)
Answer: t = 2.
Time: 45s

âœ… The Practix Way

Use \(-\frac{b}{2a}\):
\(t = -\frac{20}{2(-5)}\)
\(t = 2\)
Done.
Time: 5s

Quadratics

2. Sum of Solutions

\[ \text{Sum} = -\frac{b}{a} \]

The SAT loves to ask for the "sum of all values of x" for a quadratic equation. Most students solve the whole equation first. Don't be that student.

"What is the sum of the solutions to the equation \(3x^2 + 18x - 21 = 0\)?"

ðŸš« The School Way

Factor or use Quadratic Formula:
\(3(x^2 + 6x - 7) = 0\)
\((x+7)(x-1) = 0\)
Roots: -7, 1
Sum: -7 + 1 = -6.
Time: 40s

âœ… The Practix Way

Use \(-\frac{b}{a}\):
\(Sum = -\frac{18}{3}\)
\(Sum = -6\)
Answered in one second.
Time: 1s

3. The Discriminant

\[ D = b^2 - 4ac \]

When the question mentions "number of solutions," "no real solutions," or "exactly one solution," ignore the rest of the quadratic formula and just calculate this part.

"In the equation \(x^2 + kx + 9 = 0\), for what value of \(k\) does the equation have exactly one real solution?"

ðŸš« The Panic Mode

Trying to plug in random values for k until the equation looks "factorable."
Time: Infinite (maybe 2 mins)

âœ… The Practix Way

One solution means \(D = 0\):
\(k^2 - 4(1)(9) = 0\)
\(k^2 = 36 \rightarrow k=6\).
Time: 10s

Algebra

4. Growth & Decay

\[ y = a(1 \pm r)^t \]

The foundation of percent word problems. "Initial value" is \(a\), the percent change is \(r\), and duration is \(t\).

"A town's population of 5,000 increases by 3% every year. What will the population be after 6 years?"

Geometry

5. Circle Equation

\[ (x-h)^2 + (y-k)^2 = r^2 \]

Identify circle center \((h, k)\) and radius \(r\) instantly. Don't forget that \(r^2\) on the right means you MUST take the square root to find the actual radius.

"What is the radius of the circle defined by \((x-3)^2 + (y+5)^2 = 49\)?"

ðŸš« The Careless Error

Looking at the 49 and saying "Radius is 49." Or getting the center signs wrong.
Time: Lost Points

âœ… The Practix Way

Radius is \(\sqrt{49} = 7\).
Center is (3, -5).
Time: 2s

Algebra

6. Complementary Trig

\[ \sin(x) = \cos(90 - x) \]

If you see sine and cosine in the same question on a right triangle problem, they are almost certainly testing this identity. The sine of one acute angle is always equal to the cosine of the other.
