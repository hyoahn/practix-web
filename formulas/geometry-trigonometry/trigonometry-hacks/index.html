Complementary Trig Theorem

\[ \sin(x) = \cos(90 - x) \]

Sine and Cosine are "Co-functions". They are equal when their angles sum to 90 degrees (or \(\pi/2\) radians).

üìù Example: Basic Complement

If \(\sin(x) = 0.8\), then \(\cos(90-x)\) is also \(0.8\). No calculator needed.

üìù Example: Radian Complement

\(\sin(\pi/6) = \cos(\pi/2 - \pi/6) = \cos(\pi/3) = 0.5\).
