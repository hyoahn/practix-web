The 3 Pillars of an 800

1 Speed (Reflexes > Thought)

In school, you get credit for "showing your work." On the SAT, showing your work is a liability. It's slow.

The Practix Method: We train you to solve questions without algebra.
• Need the vertex? Don't complete the square. Use -b/2a.
• Need a line equation? Don't calculate slope. Use Desmos regression.

2 Pattern Recognition

The SAT has a limited number of standard "traps." Once you see the matrix, you can't unsee it.

The Practix Method: We don't organize questions by topic. We organize them by trap. You learn to spot the trick before you even read the full question.

3 Mental Endurance

The Digital SAT is adaptive. Module 2 is brutal. Most students lose focus at question 18. Practix builds your stamina through timed "sprints" that mimic the pressure of the real test.
