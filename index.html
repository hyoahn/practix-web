Recommended timeline: 8 weeks of prep for 100+ point improvement. 4 weeks minimum for meaningful gains.

How many practice tests should I take?

4-6 full tests is ideal. More isn't always better - quality review matters more than quantity. Each test takes 2+ hours, so budget 4+ hours including review.

Are unofficial practice tests worth it?

Mostly no. Official College Board tests match the real exam. Unofficial tests often have different difficulty, timing, or question styles that can build bad habits.

What's the difference between Bluebook and Khan?

Bluebook = Full practice tests (exam simulation). Khan Academy = Individual skill practice (targeted learning). Use both: Khan for learning, Bluebook for testing.

How does Digital SAT scoring work?

The test is adaptive: Module 2 difficulty depends on Module 1 performance. You need harder Module 2 questions to reach 750+. Each section (Reading/Math) is 200-800.

Can I improve 100+ points?

Yes, with proper strategy. Most students score below their potential due to unfamiliarity, not ability. 8 weeks of focused prep typically yields 100-150 point gains.

When should I take the SAT?

Junior year spring (March/May) is ideal - gives time to retake senior fall if needed. Take it when you have 6-8 weeks of dedicated prep time available.
